Understanding Your Landslide Risk

Landslides are among the major natural disasters that affect large parts of India, especially in the Himalayan region, Western Ghats, and Northeastern regions. They cause significant damage to infrastructure, property, and human lives every year.

The prediction is based on several factors including precipitation levels, terrain characteristics, and historical landslide data for your location. Our model analyzes these factors to determine the probability of a landslide occurring.

What Your Result Means:

  • Landslide risk exist: High probability (>80%) of landslide occurrence. Immediate precautionary measures recommended.
  • Moderate risk: Significant probability (50-80%) of landslide occurrence. Be prepared and stay alert.
  • Mild risk: Low to moderate probability (30-50%) of landslide occurrence. Stay updated with weather forecasts.
  • No landslide: Very low probability (<30%) of landslide occurrence. Normal activities can continue.

Safety Measures

1

Before a Landslide

Be aware of the landslide risk in your area. Watch for warning signs like changes in landscape, patterns of storm-water drainage, and leaning trees. Create an emergency communication plan with your family.

2

During a Landslide

If you suspect imminent landslide danger, evacuate immediately. Listen for unusual sounds like trees cracking or boulders knocking together. If evacuation is not possible, curl into a tight ball and protect your head.

3

After a Landslide

Stay away from the landslide area as there may be danger of additional slides. Check for injured or trapped people near the slide area, but do not enter the direct slide area. Report broken utility lines and damaged roadways to appropriate authorities.
